    *   **The Practice:** Don't identify with or chase every thought. When an unwholesome thought arises (e.g., "I'm lonely," "I'm not good enough"), recognize it as **Mara** (a temptation, an illusion).
    *   **The Method:** You don't have to fight the thought. **"Starve it with Sati" (mindfulness).** Acknowledge it ("I see you"), see it as empty and impersonal, and let it go by not feeding it with attention.
    *   **Cultivate the Opposite:** Replace unwholesome thoughts with wholesome ones. If the mind says "I'm lonely," remind yourself, "Everything is okay right now. I am peaceful." The ultimate wholesome view is recognizing your inherent worth: **"You are an enlightened Buddha."** Believing this changes your inner chemistry.

4.  **On Simplicity and Letting Go:**
    *   The Dharma is simple. People often complicate it because they don't want the simple truth: **let go of attachment to wandering thoughts and emotions.**
    *   Happiness is available **right now**, independent of external conditions. It's about realizing you are already in a "kingdom of heaven" if you stop following the mind's complaints and dramas.
    *   The mind is like a water buffalo on a rope‚Äîit will always wander. The practice is gently guiding it back, not expecting perfection.

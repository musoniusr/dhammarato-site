The practice is presented as a simple, moment-to-moment adjustment:

    *   A parable is shared: A wise farmer sees a storm (negative attachments) coming and returns to the safety of his cabin (the present moment/breath). The arrogant farmer stays out and suffers. The "door" to the cabin is the breath.

2.  **Calm Awareness vs. Mere Calmness:**
    *   It‚Äôs not enough to just be calm. One must be **awarely calm**‚Äîmindful of the calm state itself, its impermanence, and able to let it go without dissatisfaction when it ends.

3.  **The "Scale" or "Baseline" of the Mind:**
    *   The mind's natural, peaceful home is a balanced "zero."
    *   Practice involves checking in and gently bringing the mind back from extremes of negative emotion (-10) or excessive, clinging joy (+10) back to the peaceful, balanced center (0) via the breath.

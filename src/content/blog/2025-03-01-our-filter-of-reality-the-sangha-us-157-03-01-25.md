2.  **This Process is Flawed and Limiting:** Our perception is incomplete and biased. Our knowledge is tiny compared to the vastness of reality. We often jump to conclusions based on insufficient information, leading to misunderstanding and suffering.

3.  **The Problem of Ownership ("I, Me, Mine"):** We habitually identify with our interpretations, believing our story *is* reality (e.g., "I am a Democrat," "This problem is mine"). This ownership leads to attachment, defensiveness, and distress.

    *   **Apply "Not Me, Not Mine":** Dis-identify from your interpretations. Use reminders like "This is not my business" or "Nothing is worth clinging to as I, me, or mine" (Bhikkhu Buddhadasa) to release ownership and the suffering it brings.
    *   **Investigate, Don't Conclude:** Replace quick judgment with continuous looking and investigation. Gather more information before forming a view.
    *   **Change the Internal Narrative:** Actively replace unwholesome, anxiety-producing stories (based on old, faulty memories) with wholesome ones. The ultimate wholesome story is: "Everything is already okay." This leads to peace.
    *   **Freedom is Letting Go of Stories:** True freedom comes from not being enslaved by the feelings generated by our inaccurate internal stories. When we stop believing and clinging to them, we relax.

### Metaphors and Stories

Based on the transcript, the speaker uses several **metaphors and stories** to explain the Buddhist concept of **Salyatana** (the internal sense bases) and how perception works. Here are the key ones:

### **Core Metaphors & Analogies:**

1.  **The "Story" Metaphor:** This is the central, repeated metaphor.
    *   **What it is:** Our perception (Salyatana) is described as a **story we tell ourselves** about reality. We don't experience raw reality; we experience our internal narrative built from past memories.
    *   **Example:** "The Salyatana then can be said that it's a story that we tell ourselves, and the story is about reality."

2.  **The "Label" or "Made In" Metaphor:**
    *   **What it is:** We should mentally label our perceptions like a clothing tag that says "Made in China." We should recognize our thoughts with a stamp that says **"this shit's made up right here."**
    *   **Example:** "But very few of us go around putting a stamp on our own internal reality, said this shit's made up right here."

3.  **The "Filter" or "Process" Metaphor:**
    *   **What it is:** Perception is a **filter** (nama-rupa) that takes raw input (shape, color, sound waves) and processes it into a recognizable internal image.
    *   **Example:** Comparing it to understanding language: "You've got the English language stored... then in reality, all I'm doing is making sound waves."

4.  **The "Memory Bank/Storage" Metaphor:**
    *   **What it is:** Our past experiences and conditioning are stored in a **memory system** (Samskara), which is the database we draw from to create our "stories."
    *   **Example:** A child learning a "bad word" stores it in their "memory banks" after a reaction from their mother.

5.  **"Two Ships Passing in the Night":**
    *   **What it is:** A metaphor for how our perception often **misses reality entirely**. We are trying to make sense of the world, but our internal story and external reality can be completely disconnected.
    *   **Example:** "it's like two ships passing in the night, they don't even see each other."

6.  **"Owning" or "Claiming" as Identity:**
    *   **What it is:** We **"own"** our stories and make them part of our identity (e.g., "I am a Democrat"). This causes suffering because we then feel personally impacted by everything related to that story.
    *   **Example:** "people will watch the news and see the Republicans, and they'll either say, 'That's mine.'"

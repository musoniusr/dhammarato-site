### Summary

Based on the provided transcript, here is a summary of the key teaching from this segment of the Dhamma talk:

The speaker (likely Dhammarato) is instructing on a practical method from **Anapanasati (mindfulness of breathing)** to deal with a scattered, anxious, and uptight mind.

**Core Teaching:** When the mind is agitated and cannot stay focused, a useful technique is to intentionally begin **breathing heavily and vigorously** through the mouth. This is referred to as practicing the "short breath" stage of Anapanasati.

**Purpose:** The heavy, rhythmic breathing serves as a "reset" button for the mind and body. It:
1.  **Generates energy and heat** (as hinted by "You're not going to dry that robe if you don't get hot").
2.  **Forces the mind to engage** with a strong, present-moment physical sensation, pulling it away from distracting thoughts.
3.  **Creates group synergy** when done collectively, as the speaker actively encourages everyone to breathe in rhythm together.

**Method:** The instruction is communal and energetic. The leader demonstrates and urges participants to follow along actively with mouth breathing for a sustained period (e.g., 5 minutes).

**In essence:** This is a direct, physical intervention for mental agitation. Instead of trying to gently calm a wild mind, the technique uses vigorous breathing to unify attention, build energy, and break the cycle of anxiety and distraction.
